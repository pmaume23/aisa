About Together we can make a difference

Who we are

Our association members are part of the Ashinaga Africa Initiative (AAI), Leadership program; (Ashinaga is an NGO that gives educational and emotional support for the Japanese; and Sub Saharan African scholars to study abroad. Our members consist of scholars and graduates dedicated to bringing a positive change to Africa. Majority of the members are offering various majors in different universities across the globe.

What we do

  • We facilitate professional development through online webinars with special guest speakers from the professional field. We aim to enhance our leadership, entrepreneurial skills, networks and diversify our knowledge about the world and its operations.
  • We work on proposals we call 'kokorozashi's' (​志​). These proposals have a deep focus on the development of Africa. Member's projects are very diverse in their fields ranging from; Entrepreneurship, Business, Agriculture, Architecture, Design, Innovation, Engineering, Education, Psychology, Medicine, Health, Environment, IT, Humanities/ liberals arts among others.
  • Connect members to opportunities across the globe.
  • Collaborate with Ashinaga in the organisation of international conferences and events like Tsudoi (集い)
  • Engage in fundraising activities (Bokin, ​募金​) for the Ashinaga foundation.
  • Provide a convenient platform for members to engage, communication and collaborate on their projects and many other interests they have.
  • Support the new AAI scholars arriving at their Universities across the globe.

History

AISA was founded in 2016 as a brain child of Ozakiさん. Its main objectives at that time included to get AAI scholars active in Bokin, and to promote cultural exchange between Africa and Japan. AISA has since grown in size and scope of activities. From 2017 to 2019, AISA became an Information centre sharing opportunites amongst members. In 2020, efforts were mainly focused on professional skills development and understanding options available after undergraduate studies. Up until this time, activities where mainly concentrated in Japan, however,As of October 2020, AISA expanded its boarders to inco-operate more scholars from France, Portugal, USA, UK and Australia. Our population todate (September 2020), approximates 200 with roughly 4 scholars from each of the 49 sub-Saharan countries in Africa. Having brought the AISA family together, on one platform, we are now pushing towards joint collaborative efforts to implement some of the projects we have been working to iprove our motherland
